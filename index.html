WHAT IS DEGROWTH?

Our dominant global economic system—capitalism—is dependent on constantly increasing its rate of production. Meanwhile, high-income nations have long surpassed a point where “growing the economy” has positive effects on human welfare.

Take Costa Rica for example: outperforming the U.S. in all levels of social scores (education, healthcare, nutrition, sanitation, life expectancy, etc.) with less than 1/5 of the per capita size of their economy.1

Furthermore, “growth” only financially benefits a small amount of the population: since 2020, two thirds of all new wealth we have collectively produced has been captured by the richest 1%—all the while 60% of the world does not yet meet their basic needs.2

Developing and growing are not the same thing. So instead of assuming that every sector of the economy needs to grow all the time–regardless of whether we actually need them to grow–degrowth advocates for “a planned downscaling of energy and resource use to bring the economy back into balance with the living world in a safe, just and equitable way”. 3

What does it promote?

Degrowth promotes economies organised around well-being instead of capital accumulation.

So instead of being made to plunder the earth to pay tribute to the rich, degrowth advocates for mandatory shorter working weeks, green job guarantees, alternative business models like Not-for-Profits4 and, most importantly, insists on stronger investment in public services like transport, education, health care and housing—it doesn’t matter what we produce but who has access to it.

Why do we need degrowth?

Capitalism is not just socially unjust, but ecologically destructive.Globally we have experienced constant economic growth in the last 100 years, but with it came a constant increase in ecological breakdown through resource usage and pollution. Never in history have we been able to produce more and use less/pollute less, nor are there predictions for the future.

Currently, growth outstrips our best efforts to decarbonise by a factor of 6.5 That means we have to decide what we want: a system change or climate change?

How can I help?

Educate yourself and get active on social media and in your community. Join or start a degrowth network where you are. The world needs you!
